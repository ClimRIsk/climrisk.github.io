The Problem

ESG scores tell you
nothing financial.

Regulators now mandate quantified climate-related financial disclosure. A colour-coded ESG score can't answer your board, your auditor, or the FCA.

📊
ESG ratings aren't financial models
MSCI, Sustainalytics, and S&P ESG scores measure disclosure quality. They cannot tell you EV at risk under a 2°C pathway — which is exactly what TCFD and IFRS S2 now require.
📍
Regional buckets miss asset reality
Two facilities in the same country can have 10× different flood exposure based on elevation and coastal distance. Regional scores mask the differences that actually drive insurance pricing and capex decisions.
📋
TCFD, IFRS S2 and CSRD need numbers
EU, UK and APAC regulators now require scenario analysis with quantified financial impact, transition risk metrics, and physical hazard data — not narrative statements.

Why we built this

Climate risk disclosure became mandatory.
The process stayed manual and expensive.

Every regulator on the planet now requires the same thing: quantified climate risk — in financial terms. TCFD. IFRS S2. CSRD E1. BRSR Core. The question is no longer whether your organisation reports climate risk. The question is how long it takes, how much it costs, and whether the output actually holds up to scrutiny.

The honest answer today is that traditional climate and ESG projects require large teams of specialists — data scientists, financial modellers, sector experts, disclosure writers — working across fragmented workflows for 8 to 16 weeks per engagement. For the organisations that can afford institutional ESG platforms charging $40,000–$200,000 a year, that infrastructure already exists. For everyone else, the process is still manual, slow, inconsistent, and inaccessible.

ClimRisk was built to change that. Not by building another reporting template. By building an engine. One that combines climate science, financial analytics, and risk intelligence into a unified, automated system — so that the analysis that used to require a team of eight analysts working for three months can be produced in hours, not weeks, for a price that doesn't require a VC round to justify.

CRI is climate-financial intelligence and workflow automation. Our platform integrates GPS-resolved hazard physics, NGFS scenario modelling, enterprise DCF, and multi-framework disclosure generation into a single pipeline — producing faster, more consistent, and decision-ready outputs for enterprises, financial institutions, consultants, and sustainability teams. By reducing dependency on large operational teams and accelerating complex analytical workflows, CRI enables organisations to improve reporting efficiency, strengthen strategic planning, and scale climate risk analysis across their entire portfolio.

Methodology

Four layers of science.
One auditable output.

Every number in a CRI report traces back to a published, peer-reviewed data source. No black box. No "proprietary AI". The calibration weights are documented and available to every client and their auditor — no NDA required.

1
Input Layer
Site Physics
GPS coordinates resolve to real-world physical attributes — elevation above sea level, coastal proximity, river network distance, land cover class, and urban heat island intensity.
SRTM DEM v3 (NASA/USGS) — elevation
OSM Coastline 2024 — coastal distance
HydroSHEDS v1.0 — river network
ESA CCI Land Cover 2020 — LULC
2
Risk Layer
Hazard Assessment
Ten physical hazards scored per asset across four IPCC SSP scenarios (1-2.6 through 5-8.5), calibrated to forward-looking CMIP6 model ensembles.
WRI Aqueduct 4.0 — flood & water stress
IPCC AR6 WGI Ch.11 — climate extremes
NASA NEX-GDDP CMIP6 — 21 GCMs
EM-DAT historical event validation
3
Financial Layer
Financial Quantification
Physical hazard intensity converted to financial impact: annual loss, EV at risk, carbon cost exposure, scenario-adjusted DCF, and WACC climate premium.
NGFS Phase 4 — carbon price paths
IPCC AR6 WGII Ch.16 — damage functions
TCFD Technical Supplement — scenario methodology
IMF Fiscal Monitor — WACC premium calibration
4
Output Layer
CRI Composite Rating
Three scored pillars combined into a 0–100 composite, mapped to an A–E letter rating calibrated against sector peers and regulatory disclosure thresholds.
Physical Risk — 35% weight
Transition Risk — 35% weight
Financial Impact — 30% weight
Sector peer calibration (MSCI, EM-DAT)

About ClimRisk

Climate intelligence.

ClimRisk is a climate intelligence and risk analytics company focused on automating complex climate and sustainability workflows through its proprietary CRI Engine. We help organisations reduce the time, cost, and human effort required for climate risk assessment, ESG analysis, scenario modelling, and sustainability reporting.

Traditional climate and ESG projects often require large teams of specialists working for weeks or months across data collection, risk analysis, financial modelling, and disclosure preparation. ClimRisk was built to streamline and automate these workflows through a scalable, technology-driven approach — combining climate science, financial analytics, and risk intelligence into a unified system that generates faster, more consistent, and decision-ready outputs.

We serve enterprises, financial institutions, consultants, and sustainability teams across mining, energy, chemicals, real estate, infrastructure, and financial services — currently in our pioneer client phase, working directly with organisations to refine the engine against real-world regulatory submissions.

Every workflow the CRI Engine performs — data collection, hazard scoring, financial modelling, disclosure structuring — would take a specialist team 8–16 weeks manually. CRI produces it in hours.
