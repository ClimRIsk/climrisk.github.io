The Problem

ESG scores tell you
nothing financial.

Regulators now mandate quantified climate-related financial disclosure. A colour-coded ESG score can't answer your board, your auditor, or the FCA.

📊
ESG ratings aren't financial models
MSCI, Sustainalytics, and S&P ESG scores measure disclosure quality. They cannot tell you EV at risk under a 2°C pathway — which is exactly what TCFD and IFRS S2 now require.
📍
Regional buckets miss asset reality
Two facilities in the same country can have 10× different flood exposure based on elevation and coastal distance. Regional scores mask the differences that actually drive insurance pricing and capex decisions.
📋
TCFD, IFRS S2 and CSRD need numbers
EU, UK and APAC regulators now require scenario analysis with quantified financial impact, transition risk metrics, and physical hazard data — not narrative statements.

About ClimRisk

Built by practitioners,
for decision-makers.

ClimRisk was founded with a single conviction: climate risk is financial risk, and the tools to quantify it should be accessible to every enterprise — not just the handful of institutions that can afford a $40,000/year ESG licence.

We built the CRI (Climate Risk Intelligence) engine from the ground up — combining open scientific datasets (WRI Aqueduct, NGFS, IPCC AR6, NASA NEX-GDDP) with institutional-grade financial modelling to produce the kind of analysis that previously required a team of analysts and months of work.

We are currently in our pilot engagement phase — working directly with a small number of enterprise clients across mining, energy, chemicals, and financial services to refine the engine, build case studies, and validate the output against real regulatory submissions.

Rigour over narrative
Every number in a CRI report traces back to a peer-reviewed source. We cite IPCC AR6, WRI Aqueduct 4.0, and NGFS Phase 4 — not proprietary black-box models.
Site-specific, not regional
Two assets 40km apart in the same country can have radically different physical risk profiles. Our GPS hazard engine computes from coordinates, not administrative regions.
Transparent data, proprietary calibration
All data sources are fully cited — WRI Aqueduct, NGFS, IPCC AR6. The calibration and weighting layer that turns raw hazard scores into financial exposure is proprietary and documented for client review under NDA.
Methodology

ClimRisk's integration methodology is documented for client review under NDA. Data sources are fully cited; the calibration and weighting layer is proprietary.
