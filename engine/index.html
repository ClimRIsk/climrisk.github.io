Methodology

The CRI Engine.

Four layers. GPS hazard physics to composite climate rating. Every calculation traces to a published source.

See validation results →Download methodology PDF →
01

GPS Hazard Physics

Real spatial data. Not lookup tables.

Data sources

WRI Aqueduct 4.0
NASA NEX-GDDP
IPCC AR6 WG1 Ch11
FEMA flood zones
Global Cyclone Risk Model

Asset coordinates are resolved against WRI Aqueduct 4.0 for water stress, NASA NEX-GDDP for temperature and precipitation, and IPCC AR6 WG1 hazard atlases for 25 hazard types. The result is an asset-specific hazard probability path from 2026 to 2050 under each SSP scenario.

Outputs

→Annual production loss fraction per hazard
→Decade-by-decade trajectory
→Asset vulnerability multipliers by equipment type
02

Climate Scenarios

NGFS Phase 4. Three pathways.

Data sources

NGFS Phase 4 (2023)
IIASA scenario database
IEA WEO 2024
Wood Mackenzie consensus

Three NGFS Phase 4 scenarios define the policy and physical environment: Net Zero 2050 (front-loaded transition), Delayed Transition (policy lag then emergency repricing), and Current Policies (physical risk dominant). Each scenario carries a carbon price path, commodity demand curve, and physical hazard trajectory.

Outputs

→Carbon price path 2026–2050 ($/tCO₂e)
→Commodity demand index by sector
→Physical hazard intensity multipliers
03

Financial Quantification

Every hazard becomes a dollar number.

Data sources

CDP disclosed emissions data
Wood Mackenzie commodity prices
EU ETS registry
MSCI peer financials

For each asset and each scenario year: revenue from volume × price, opex with carbon-inflated energy cost, physical loss cost from hazard-disrupted production margin, carbon cost from Scope 1+2 EU ETS exposure, transition capex from abatement MACC, and stranded asset writedowns when price falls below full unit cost.

Outputs

→Annual FCF trajectory per scenario
→Physical loss cost by hazard (€)
→Carbon cost as EU ETS exposure (€)
→Stranded asset writedowns (€)
04

Composite CRI Rating

A–E. Backed by three pillar scores.

Data sources

MSCI ESG benchmark (47 companies)
Moody's climate VAR study
S&P Global CSA ratings
Academic TCFD validation literature

The composite CRI score (0–100) weights three pillars: Physical Risk (avg annual loss / revenue across scenarios), Transition Risk (carbon burden + EBITDA compression under NZE), and Financial Impact (NPV delta NZE vs Current Policies, WACC uplift, equity VaR). DCF discounts all FCF streams at climate-adjusted WACC.

Outputs

→CRI Rating A–E
→Composite score 0–100
→Physical / Transition / Financial pillar scores
→Enterprise and equity value at risk
